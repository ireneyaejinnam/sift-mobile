# Sift — VC pitch script (8-minute target)

**Total: 8:00.** Slides 5:00 · live demo 2:00 · close + buffer 1:00.

Open `presentation/sift-vc-deck.html` in Chrome, hit `F` to fullscreen. Phone on AirPlay/mirror, Discover tab loaded with a fresh-ish taste profile, Plan tab pre-seeded with one Going event. Volume up.

---

## SLIDE 1 — Title (15s)

> "I'm Jary. This is **Sift** — the NYC event app that actually knows what you like.
> 8 minutes. The last two are a live demo on a real phone, with a real catalog, swiped by a real user — me."

*(advance)*

## SLIDE 2 — Problem (40s)

> "We talked to twenty young professionals in NYC. Same complaint, every time. This is Sam, twenty-three: *'We spend most of our time looking into activities we end up not even going to. We just get overwhelmed and give up.'*
> 
> Eighty-seven percent of them use three or more apps to find things to do. The number-one pain point we heard, in their words: *'too many options, not enough good ones.'* That weekend Sam stayed home. **Discovery cost them the night.**"

*(advance)*

## SLIDE 3 — Insight (30s)

> "The category has been a fight between **volume** — Eventbrite, Dice — and **editorial** — TimeOut, the Infatuation. Both lose. Volume drowns you. Editorial is static — you read the list once and forget.
> 
> Sift is the third thing: **taste**. Curated *and* personalized *and* active. You don't read it. It gets to know you."

*(advance)*

## SLIDE 4 — Solution (30s)

> "Three words: **curates, swipes, learns.** Discover the right events, plan them in seconds, and the app sharpens every weekend. Three behaviors. One app."

*(advance)*

## SLIDE 5 — PROOF / the aha slide (45s) **← this is where you sell that it's real**

> "Before the demo — a lot of you have seen pitches with mock data. Sift is shipping today.
> 
> *(point at numbers)* **One hundred and twenty hand-qualified NYC events live in production right now.** Refreshed weekly. **Eighty-five percent** of what we ingest gets rejected by an AI rubric and a human reviewer. We pull from **six** of the best taste sources in the city. And **seventeen real users** are swiping right now — plus two more this week, organic.
> 
> The hardest part of an event app — a curated catalog of a city — is *already done.* The next ninety seconds prove the loop on top of it works."

*(advance — keep tempo, this is the credibility moment)*

## SLIDE 6 — Edge 1, Catalog (40s)

> "Two edges. First: the catalog *is* the moat. Eventbrite ingests everything because their model **needs** a firehose — cutting eighty-five percent would crater their business. They structurally can't do what we do.
> 
> An AI rubric scores every event: audience fit, venue quality, verified dates, real prices, no fabricated listings. A human editor reviews the keep-pile. The constraint **is** the product — if it's on Sift, it's worth your Saturday."

*(advance)*

## SLIDE 7 — Edge 2, Algorithm (40s)

> "Second edge: the deck re-ranks in **real time.** No nightly batch, no cold start.
> 
> Three signals tune it. *(read each)* Swipe drops or boosts. The feedback sheet retunes whole categories. And anything you save or mark Going becomes a beacon — similar events climb to the top in front of you. That last part is easier to show than to explain."

## SLIDE 8 — Demo transition (5s)

> "Let me show you."

*(pick up phone — switch to mirror — Discover tab is already up)*

## DEMO — 90 to 120s, four beats

### Beat 1 — "The cold deck." (15s)
> "I open Sift. One card. Tap for detail, swipe to react. Right now my taste profile is intentionally light — this is roughly the cold-start experience."

### Beat 2 — "Swipe right = going." (20s)
*(swipe right on a strong card — Demi Lovato, Knicks, Sabrina Carpenter, whatever's queued)*
> "Demi Lovato at MSG. Big fan. Swipe right.
> Three things just happened. Marked Going. Saved to my Plan tab. And the system pushed more pop and arena shows up the deck."

### Beat 3 — "Swipe left + undo." (15s)
*(swipe left on a niche card)*
> "Niche jazz trio — not my Saturday. Swipe left. Notice the **undo** button in the corner — accidental swipes are recoverable. Tinder doesn't even let you do that without paying."

### Beat 4 — "Explicit signal — watch the deck reorder." (30s)
*(open the feedback sheet on the active card)*
> "When I want to send a stronger signal — *more of this whole vibe* — I open the feedback sheet. **More like this.**
> *(close sheet, start swiping through next 3 cards — they should now visibly lean into the chosen vibe)*
> "Watch — every music card in the deck just reordered. That's the loop. Every signal — swipe, save, button — re-ranks what comes next, in real time."

### Optional Beat 5 — "Plan it." (15s) — only if pacing allows
*(switch to Plan tab → tap the Demi Lovato event → Add to Calendar)*
> "Tap Plan. There's the Demi Lovato show. One tap — Add to Calendar. iPhone calendar with the venue address. Done."

*(switch back to slides — advance)*

## Q&A — likely 30-second answers

**"How is this different from Eventbrite?"** — *Eventbrite shows you everything. Discovery is your problem. We reject 85%. Their model can't.*

**"Why no friend layer?"** — *Two reasons. Our user research said friend graphs add friction, not value. And every event app that's tried social has died bootstrapping the network. We bet on taste over network.*

**"Defensibility?"** — *The taste graph compounds. Every swipe by every user makes the next swipe smarter. By month six the model is the moat — not the catalog.*

**"How do you scale to a second city?"** — *Same rubric, new editorial lead. The taste system is city-agnostic. The hard problem is finding the right curator per city, not the technology.*

**"What stops Eventbrite copying?"** — *They can't reject 85% — their economic model is the firehose. They'd kill their business doing what we do.*

**"What about cold-start?"** — *We solve it three ways. The catalog is pre-curated, so cold users still get a strong deck. We use category prior signals from onboarding. And the model warms in roughly twenty swipes — which is one session.*
