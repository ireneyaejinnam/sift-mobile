## SLIDE 2 — Problem + Sam's quote (45 sec)

> "We talked to 20 people in our target audience — young professionals in NYC. The same complaint kept coming up. This is Sam, 23: *'We spend most of our time looking into activities that we end up not even going to anymore. We just get overwhelmed and kind of give up.'*
>
> Sam isn't unusual. **87% of our users use three or more platforms** to find things to do. The number-one pain point we heard? *'Too many options, not enough good ones.'*
>
> NYC has more events than any other city on earth. Discovery is somehow worse than it's ever been."

*(Click forward.)*

## SLIDE 3 — Solution (30 sec)

> "Sift's solution is three simple words: **curates, swipes, learns.**
>
> Sift isn't another aggregator dumping ten thousand events on you. It's a taste-maker — built and tuned for you, one swipe at a time.
>
> Three behaviors in one app: **Discover** the right events. **Plan** them in seconds. And the app **learns** as you go — so tomorrow's deck looks nothing like today's."

*(Click forward.)*

---

## SLIDE 4 — Edge #1: Curated Catalog (60 sec)

> "Our first edge is the **catalog itself.**
>
> Most event apps try to win on volume. Eventbrite has ten thousand NYC listings every week. Sift goes the other way — we ingest everything from Ticketmaster, Eater, The Infatuation, TimeOut, Resy Hit List, and Dice — and **we reject 85 percent of it.**
>
> An AI rubric vets every event for audience fit, venue quality, verified dates and prices, no fabricated listings — and a human reviews before it ships. The result: a hundred and twenty-plus hand-qualified NYC events, refreshed weekly. Zero underground filler.
>
> The constraint *is* the product. If it's on Sift, it's worth your Saturday."

*(Click forward.)*

---

## SLIDE 5 — Edge #2: Algorithm That Learns (60 sec)

> "Our second edge is what happens after the curation. The algorithm learns your taste in real time.
>
> Three signals tune the deck:
>
> Swipe **left or right** drops or saves the event, and nudges the vibe. Tap **'More like this'** or **'Not my thing'** for explicit category tuning. And when you save or mark Going, similar events get boosted to the top.
>
> No nightly batch. No cold waiting period. The deck re-ranks the instant you tap. That's the magic — and it's easier to show than to explain."

*(Click forward.)*

## DEMO SCRIPT (90–120 sec)

**Setup before going on stage:**
- Open Sift, log in, **make sure you're on the Discover tab** with a fresh deck loaded
- Have a few specific events queued near the top (the Demi Lovato concert, a Broadway show, a niche-jazz pick) — if your taste profile is reasonably untrained this should be the case
- Volume up so the haptic/swipe sounds register

### The four-beat demo

#### Beat 1 — "Here's the deck." *(15 sec)*
> "When I open Sift, I see one card at a time. Tap it for detail, swipe to react. Right now I have no taste profile — this is the cold deck."

*Action:* Show the active card. Read the title aloud. Don't swipe yet.

#### Beat 2 — "Swipe right means going." *(20 sec)*
> "Demi Lovato's at MSG this weekend. I'm a fan. **I swipe right.**"

*Action:* Swipe right on the card. The "Going" indicator flashes; the next card slides in.

> "That just did three things. Marked the event as Going. Saved it to my Plan tab. And told the system to push more pop and arena shows up the deck."

#### Beat 3 — "Swipe left to dismiss." *(20 sec)*
> "Now here's a niche jazz trio. Not my Saturday night. **Swipe left.**"

*Action:* Swipe left on the next card. Note: the **Undo** icon appears in the top-right of the next card.

> "Notice that little undo button that appeared in the corner — if I swipe by accident, I tap that and the event comes back. Tinder doesn't even let you undo without paying."

#### Beat 4 — "Explicit category tuning." *(30 sec)*
> "And when I want to send a stronger signal — *less of this whole vibe* — I open the feedback sheet."

*Action:* Long-press the active card or tap the feedback button → BottomSheet opens with "More like this" / "Not my thing".

> "Tap **More like this** — every music card in the deck just got reordered. **Watch the deck.**"

*Action:* Close the sheet. Swipe through the next 2–3 cards. The new top picks should now lean obviously toward music / similar vibe.

> "That's the loop. Every signal — swipe, save, button — re-ranks what comes next, in real time."

#### Beat 5 (optional, if pacing allows) — "Plan it." *(15 sec)*
> "Tap over to **Plan**. There's Demi Lovato from a minute ago. One tap — **Add to Calendar** — and it's in my iPhone calendar with the venue address."

*Action:* Switch to Plan tab → show the Going event → tap Add to Calendar → iOS calendar prompts.

*(Switch back to projected slides.)*

## Q&A prep — likely questions + 30-sec answers

**Q: How is this different from Eventbrite / TimeOut?**
> *Eventbrite shows you everything — discovery is your problem. TimeOut writes editorial — you read it once and forget. Sift is the only thing that's curated **and** personalized **and** active. You don't read it; it gets to know you.*

**Q: Why no friend layer?**
> *Two reasons. First, our user interviews told us friend graphs add friction, not value — people want curation, not another social feed. Second, every event app that's tried social has died trying to bootstrap the network. We bet on taste over network.*

**Q: How do you scale beyond NYC?**
> *Same rubric, new city, new editorial curator. The taste-learning system is city-agnostic. Brooklyn → LA or SF is the next milestone. The hard problem is finding the right curator per city, not the technology.*

**Q: How do you make money?**
> *Three revenue paths we're exploring: (1) booking referrals from Resy / Ticketmaster, (2) creator-curated lists with sponsored placement, (3) freemium where free users get ten daily picks and pro users get unlimited + early access to drops.*

**Q: What stops Eventbrite from copying you?**
> *They can't. Their economic model needs the firehose. Cutting 85% of their inventory would crater their business. We're a wedge they structurally can't move on.*
